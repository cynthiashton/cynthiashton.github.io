Giri is the concept in which relationships are tied with responsibilities depending on the status of the persons of the relationship. Like all cultures, Japanese culture is dynamic and unique in the structure of relationships. These structures take into account both vertical and horizontal type relationships, seeing both how individuals relate to one's self above, below and as a peer, setting specific rules which govern interactions. Kurosawa's Thrown of Blood displayed feudal Japan and more so the art of the warrior spirit. Mizoguchi's Sisters of Gion expressed the battle between traditional and modern individual concepts. The development of characters in Ozu's Equinox Flower shows the family dynamics of Japan. Finally after all the years of observing Japanese culture

In general, all of the films grasped at many aspects of Japanese culture, society and art. Perhaps it may be because I am not a true film critic that I never deeply analyzed or critically view films. In Takeshi Kitano's genda-geki film, of Kids Return displays the restless rebellious youth of modern day Japan. When thinking of the message of the film, I agree with Takeshi that growing into adulthood in Japan is challenging for it give very little opportunity to let one freely choose what to do.

Description: This course will introduce students to Japanese experience of modernity expressed in personal narratives, a special type of literature. The themes will include the Meiji restoration, the war, women's position, social class, minorities, subculture and other aspects of Japan. Artifact: This is my final paper for this course, it is an analysis of femininity within modern literature and reflections to Japanese societies.

Females and Japan

            Growing up as a Japanese and American I was always questioned about my identity. Although it was very clear to me who I am, society's constant pressures of which category I choose brought question to my identity. Since I grew up mostly in America with an American education I lacked in Japanese knowledge. As a result I decided to move to Japan and study thoroughly of the Japanese language, culture, history, and society. Taking this Modern Japanese Literature course brought me into a deeper understanding of Japan and its development. Even though I knew of modern day Japan, I didn't understand the process of its history which made it what it is today. Through autobiographies, memoirs, and life writings I began to comprehend the patriarchal society along with extreme female issues.  Even though the Meiji era introduced the concepts of rights, the gender roles maintain feudal thinking due to the twenty six hundred years of traditional customs. Female issues are deeply rooted from the Eastern Asian and feudal traditions. Salvation for women was neglected throughout the development of Japan from the Meiji era.

            The Japanese language itself preserves the traditional values of feudalism.  Separated into masculine and feminine forms of vocabulary, the language is reflected of the culture. Often times, people tell me that I do not speak onnarashii because of the way I say "you" or "I".  Till this day the separation of speech is used within today's society and the people of Japan see nothing sexist about it. The separation of speech by male and female reflects the societal values of collectivism. By separating creates a group identity. This is frequently done within Japanese society with class status, region, religious cults, schooling, companies, et cetera. Separation of genders is still practice in Eastern cultures. This is why the language, education, society status, and everything that comes with culture is framed with the Eastern perspective of division of the sexes. The education is separated from feudal times, which framed the status in women.

 The Great Learning for Women restrained women suffrage and the status of Japanese women. The Great Learning for Women, also known as Onna Daigaku, was written in the mid 16th century by Kaibara Ekiken who was strongly influenced by Confucianism beliefs. Onna Daigaku is the education that all Japanese women received since the creation of the book. Onna Daigaku states that "... it is a girl's destiny, on reaching womanhood, to go to a new home and live in submission to her parents-in-law..." (Theodore, 230). It further states that the husband is the woman's lord and he can divorce his wife for seven outrages reasons. Women are inferior to men and it is wrongful "for her [to] by an excess of care [or] to try to attract attention to herself" (Theodore, 233).  Apparently women are born with these vicious personalities and must be tamed. Because of this, women pollute men and they must be kept separately. A woman's purpose is to reproduce therefore it is her duty to be educated in becoming a wise wife and good mother. Onna Daigaku institutionalized the belief that women are inferior to men, along with reinforces the insecurities of women, and especially separating males and females.  This feudal education created the fountain of the status of women.

             The elite started the revolution; the Meiji abandoned some traditions and preserved others. In particularly the status of women went unchanged. Even though the Meiji changed the entire class system, taxation, creation of prefectures and governors, abandonment of swords, increased import/export, many other changes and introduced new philosophies and ways of thinking. With all this change happening, the elite males made the constitution in which: women were prohibited by law to the right to vote, the right to join political parties, and the right on expressing political values. Even though with the concept of rights were being practice the males felt that women are not human. Or so does the constitution reflects, that women are not consider human. The Eastern Asian perspective does not consider it be inhuman to treat women as they have been treated for centuries. Women are used to be the pride of the nation. "Westerners lauding Japanese women's doll-like feminine charms serve as bonds strengthening native weapons of control" (Ishimoto, 278). I agree with Ishimoto that the nation of Japan use women as a symbol of preservation of Japanese culture. Women in Japan experienced especially in marriage.

            Japan's concept of marriage built a foundation of inequality. The Eastern Asian perspective of marriage is to preserve status not an acknowledgment of love. "These marriages were arranged primarily for political purposes, interweaving families of wealth and power to enhance glory of to reaffirm steadfast connection" (Ishimoto, 49). When the Western thinking influenced Japan of romance, women began to have an appetite for this enchanted ideology. Within the home women were imprisoned from freedom along with enduring nuisance of concubines. Onna Daigaku states that women wait on hand and foot serving their husbands. "Women were never to go beyond that door except on formal occasions when they received permission" (Ishimoto, 48). Women had no freedom in the house, for the assignments of the days were already decided because of holidays, seasons, or family occasions kept them busy. Wealthy families would be so busy they would have the help of "maids". The concubines, or maids, were separated into two classifications.  "...'the Honorable Pure' and 'the Honorable Impure' were the two classifications into which the young women-in-waiting were divided" (Ishimoto, 48). The pure were untouched by the husband and the impure were the ones who already been touched.  Western thinking also started the one husband one wife system. Prior to that politicians like Matsukata Masayoshi had not only just a wife but three concubines (Reischauer, 103). According to Onna Daigaku, women had no right to speak against such animosity. The concubines came from "respectable families, willing to let their daughter assume the secondary role in a more prominent family" (Reischauer, 103). Women were given off to families of wealth who needed more servants in the household. The transformation of the concept of marriage is still in struggle. The practice of concubines may no longer legally be allowed but the practice of sexulization of women is very much alive.

The practice of geisha has transformed into a degretting businesses such as the sex industry, soapland, kyabakura, and even the music industry. Concubines were considered to be higher class then a geisha. "Geisha normally came from impoverished families, which sold them into establishments where they were trained to become professional entertainers" (Reischauer, 102-103). There were geishas who were just entertainers and the lower ranking geishas where prostitutes. Geisha houses are merely just pleasure corners which politicians or respected wealthy men used to hold meetings. This was practice from the 600s, and women "…do not see anything dishonoring in selling themselves into a strange man for money, and the man sees nothing shameful in visiting these pleasure houses, an atmosphere of harmless cheerfulness prevails in these establishments..." (Ishimoto, 238). Perhaps Ishimoto is suggesting this because women do not know better for this is engrained into the Japanese society as acceptable. Because of the prohibitation of political activist of females, it was not until 1957 that prostitution became illegal. Geisha houses existence may only be in Kyoto now. The nation take great pride of these classy entertainers for it has a significant role in their history. Now the sex industry creates outrages forms of pornography, which often is found next to the Disney movies section in Book Off. Soapland is a modern brothel type business which prostitutes engage in sexual activity with their clients. Kyabakura bars, a hostess club where men can drink with the pleasure of conversation with a worker. Even in the music industry girl groups such as AKB48 are hyper sexualized, undergoing plastic surgery to create the perfect girl. I think the reason why women see no inequalities of their gender is because of the societal acceptance and demands.

The poisonous education of Onna Daigaku has polluted the society. Onna Daigaku and the government's belief of women's occupation destiny is be become a "good wife wise mother." Even with Meiji restoration requiring all to attend schooling, the government also created women's college to educate women of "good wife wise mother." Ishimoto states that important educational subjects for women are penmanship, sewing, cooking, and art. This sort of education does not liberate a mind yet just refrain it from any type of progression. This is the soul reason of why women never reach their salvation.

The Eastern perspective influenced Japan's language and feudal traditions which has great restraints for women. The Meiji era flipped Japan upside down along with preserving Japanese-ness, and used women as a symbol of feudal beliefs. Marriages origins in Japan carried great suffering for women as they shared their husbands with concubines. If not shared with concubines, the husbands would go to the red district to receive pleasure from a geisha. The faulty education for women has trapped women from liberation. Since Meiji the role of women has changed but not as progressively as Japan's society. In September I was watching television with my uncle, a special feature about women obtaining jobs usually occupied by men. Women salvation has been delayed because men are in power. Perhaps the women needed individual salvation before making their move in society. Today in Japanese society, I constantly get reminded of what's my role because of my gender. Even though I understand the root of gender specified ideology, it must be the American in me that makes me rebel against such restraints.

自身か謙遜

アメリカでは自分に自信がある人が多いです。自分がほめられた時に悪い気持ちで返事をします。ほめられた人は相手に"私はあなたより幸せだし、お金を持っているから私の方がえらい"と返事をするのが普通です。だいたいその返事は相手をいやな気持ちにします。でも、アメリカには人と場合によってほめられた時の返事は違います。たとえば、"そのカバンは高そう"　とほめて、ほめられた人は"いいえ、あんまり高くなかった"と返事をすることが多いです。本当はそのカバンは高かった。

アメリカの歴史は自分に自信を持つことが大事です。日本の歴史では謙遜をすることが大事です。日本人は謙遜をすることが大切です。日本人はほめられた時には自分の悪いことを最初に言います。そしてかならず謙遜します。たとえば、カバンをほめらったとあんまり喜んだ返事をしません。自分のほめられたものの悪いてんを言います。なぜかと言うと謙遜しているからです。日本の文化は謙遜をすることを大切にしているからでしょう。

でも、謙遜をすることには問題も。日本人が面接の時に自信を持たないから、面接を落ちる可能性もあります。それに、日本人はいつも謙遜をしていますが、本当にしているのかがわかりません。

アメリカと日本の違いは自信を持つか謙遜をするか、ということでしょう。私にはどちらも大切だと思います。なぜかと言うと、自信を持つことも、謙遜することもバランスを持って、どう使うかを知れば良いと思います。友達と働くか、同僚と働くかのような違いです。

Artifact: This is my essay in Japanese about not judging a book by it's cover. 

本の見た目

アメリカは移住者の国である。本の見た目で判断しない方がいいと言うアメリカの言葉があります。その意味は、人は見た目よりもっと中身の方が大切ということです。アメリカの人口は種類豊富ですが、異文化交流の誤解が多いのは当然のことでしょう。人類は知らないことに恐れと防衛本能があると思います。アメリカにはこんな機会がたくさんあるでしょう。だから、簡単に人が人を裁かない方がいいと思います。

子供のときに友達が家に遊びに来た後に、母にその友達はいい人かどうか聞きました。常に母の答えは、始めて会ったのだから中身は良く知らないからいい人かどうか分からない、ということでした。母は否めない事実を言いました。つまり、人は初めて会うときだけで判断しない方がいいでしょう。その人の人となりを心得て、判断するのが いいでしょう。

留学したとき、「本の見た目」の事件が起きました。日本語の山下先生はとても気が強い人でした。生徒たちは恐怖で気も狂わんばかりでした。授業に行くとぞっとするような気持ちが強かったのです。ある日、突然あることに気がつきました。山下先生が厳しい理由は、怖く日本語を教えると生徒がよく覚えるからです。怒られるのが怖いから生徒たちはちゃんと勉強をすることでしょう。山下先生が生徒のことを考えてくれたので、生徒たちの日本語がよくなりました。学期の終わりにやっと山下先生の優しさが分かりました。思ったより、彼女の人となりは偉かったのです。

やはり、簡単に見た目だけで人を判断しない 方がいいでしょう。母の言ったことを考えて、山下先生の事件みたくその人を時間をかけて理解していきたいです。本の物語の意義は終わるまで知らない、人の裁くのも同じことでしょう。

Reflection: With using American phrases it is better to write them in kanagana then putting the phrase in Japanese with explanation. My professor did not understand the phrase because I used direct translation.

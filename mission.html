Mission Statement

 My goals are to be proficient in Japanese, build upon teaching strategies, obtain a graduate degree, and finally work as an administrator in California. Ultimately, to be a contributor to our society through educating youth and changing the structure of education such as evaluating teachers to make sure education is relevant for everyone.  

I firmly believe in providing for the people of the oppressed. I carry many aspirations for the future. I am currently accepted into the Japanese Exchange Teachers program which I will be working for three years. I want to obtain my multi-subject credential and  a masters degree in literacy. I want to attend a Yoga Program in India, study Spanish in Chile, obtain a TELSO certificate, and pass the Japanese proficiency exam. I would like to work as an elementary school teacher, and work within the local communities at community centers. I would aspire to create programs within my district concerning bilingual children and community building. Yet the greatest ambition I have is to sit on a unified school district board and implement change that is most relevant. When I retire, I plan to create children books and leveled reading history books for primary education.

Miss Teen San Francisco 2009

In 2009 my mother encouraged me participate in the Miss Teen San Francisco beauty pageant in which I won Miss Congeniality. My contribution toward feminism.
